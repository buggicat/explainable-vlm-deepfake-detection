TASK

INVESTIGATION PROTOCOL  (follow this order — do not skip phases)

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IMAGE-TYPE TOOL MENU  (select the row matching Phase 1)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FACE / PORTRAIT
  ① ELA boundary ratio — re-compress to JPEG q=75 (cv2.imencode + cv2.imdecode),
      compute abs(original − recompressed). Report mean interior ELA, mean
      boundary ELA, and ratio. Ratio > 2.5 → splice or diffusion boundary.
  ② FFT mid-high diagonal energy ratio — numpy.fft.fft2 on luminance.
      Sum energy in ±45° diagonal bands vs. H+V bands. Real images ≈ 0.45–0.55;
      AI images often outside this range. (Most robust to JPEG — cite B-Free.)
  ③ Noise residual spatial uniformity — subtract scipy.ndimage.gaussian_filter
      (img, σ=2). Compute std of residual per 32×32 block. Unnaturally uniform
      map (std of block-stds < 2.0) → generative model fingerprint.

SCENE / LANDSCAPE / OBJECT
  ① Illumination gradient consistency — cv2.Sobel on grayscale. Compare gradient
      direction histograms across 4 quadrants. Real lighting → entropy > 3.5 bits.
  ② Local entropy map — skimage.filters.rank.entropy(img_gray, disk(5)).
      Flag regions with entropy < 2.0 (over-smooth) or > 7.5 (anomalous noise).
  ③ Wavelet HH subband energy — pywt.dwt2 at level 2. Compute HH/(LL+LH+HL+HH).
      Diffusion models suppress HH — ratio < 0.02 is suspicious for real cameras.

SUSPECTED INPAINTING / SPLICING
  ① Boundary Laplacian sharpness — cv2.Laplacian on grayscale. Compare mean
      gradient magnitude at suspected boundary vs. interior. Ratio > 3.0 → splice.
  ② Block DCT statistics — 8×8 numpy DCT. Mean and std of AC coefficients
      across blocks. Double-compressed / inpainted regions show anomalous spikes.
  ③ EXIF / metadata — PIL.Image._getexif() or piexif. Missing metadata or
      metadata mismatching pixel dimensions is forensically significant.

GENERAL (always valid; use when type is ambiguous or as verification tool)
  ○ Mid-high diagonal FFT energy — most JPEG-robust cue for any image type.
  ○ Histogram uniformity + local entropy — fast authenticity screen.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONFIDENCE FORMULA  (mandatory — prevents overconfidence and calibration drift)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
`confidence` = P(image is FAKE), range 0.0–1.0.
  0.0 = certain genuine photograph
  0.5 = maximum uncertainty (coin flip)
  1.0 = certain AI-generated / manipulated

Compute explicitly:
  confidence = clamp( 0.5 + Σ influence_i ,  0.0,  1.0 )

where each influence_i is the signed shift from one evidence item:
  "+N.NN toward fake" → add N.NN
  "−N.NN toward fake" → subtract N.NN

Example: +0.20, +0.15, −0.05, +0.10 → 0.5 + 0.40 = 0.90 → classification: fake

Show this arithmetic in your Phase 4 prose BEFORE writing the JSON.
If arithmetic conflicts with intuition, trust the arithmetic — re-examine the evidence.

HARD CONSTRAINTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Every numeric result in evidence[].result must come from code you actually ran.
  If obtained by visual inspection, set tool to "visual observation" — not a tool name.
- ≤ 5 code snippets total. Provide 3–6 evidence items. Quality over quantity.
- decision_pathway must have exactly 4 entries (one per phase).
- confidence must equal clamp(0.5 + Σ influences, 0.0, 1.0). Show the arithmetic.
- BURDEN OF PROOF: the null hypothesis is that the image is REAL. To classify as
  fake, at least one evidence item must have |influence| ≥ 0.20 (a strong signal).

  accumulate do not constitute proof. Forensic tools produce false positives on real
  images; do not let small signals add up to an unwarranted fake verdict.
- alternative_hypothesis must be specific and falsifiable — not "I could be wrong."
- plain_language_summary: zero jargon. No "ELA", "FFT", "GAN", "diffusion model".
- NEVER write "I cannot determine" anywhere. Lower confidence toward 0.5 instead
  and name the specific limiting factor.
- The fenced JSON block must be syntactically valid and the last content in your response.
